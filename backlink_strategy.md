## Phase 3: The "Asset" Strategy (Link Bait)
We already built the perfect "Link Bait" — your **Prompt Library** (`/prompts`).

1. Search Google for articles like: *"Best Google Flow Prompts"* or *"How to make cinematic AI videos"*.
2. Find the author's email or Twitter.
3. Send them a message: 
   > *"Hey [Name], loved your article on AI video generation. I just put together a massive free library of copy-and-paste cinematic prompts specifically for Google Flow. Thought it might be a cool resource to link to for your readers since it includes the exact camera angles and lighting setups. Cheers!"*

Bloggers are lazy. If you give them a cool resource to link out to, they will take it.

## Phase 4: YouTube "Tool Mention" Outreach
YouTubers who talk about AI filmmaking (like *Matt Wolfe*, *Tim Simmons / theoretically media*, etc.) need content.

1. Email them or DM them on Twitter.
2. Give them the **Pro version for free**.
3. Say: *"I built a tool that automates Google Flow so you don't have to sit there clicking generate all night. It batch processes 100 prompts at once. Let me know if you want to try it out for a video."*
4. If they make a video, their YouTube description link is a highly valuable backlink that drives massive traffic.

---

## 🛑 What NOT To Do (How to avoid Google Penalties)
- **Do not buy Fiverr backlink packages.** (10,000 backlinks for $5). These are spam and will get your site permanently removed from Google.
- **Do not spam comment sections.** (e.g., leaving "Great post! Visit auto-flow.studio" on random blogs). Google ignores these (they are "no-follow").
