# 📹 The 90-Second Daily Viral Strategy

Making a 1:30 video every single day is the absolute fastest way to get thousands of users. But to avoid burnout and keep the algorithm happy, you need a **system**, not just random videos.

## ⏱️ The 90-Second Framework
*Every single video you make should follow this exact timing structure to keep people from swiping away.*

**Phase 1: The Visual Hook (0:00 - 0:05)**
- **What to show:** The absolutely coolest AI video you generated that day (e.g., an epic cinematic shot).
- **What to say:** "Here is how I generated 50 of these cinematic videos while I was taking a shower."

**Phase 2: The Pain Point (0:05 - 0:20)**
- **What to show:** A split screen or quick cut showing the "old way" (clicking generate, waiting, clicking download, waiting). 
- **What to say:** "Normally, using Google Flow takes forever. You paste a prompt, wait 5 minutes, download it, and repeat. It's exhausting."

**Phase 3: The Superpower (0:20 - 0:60)**
- **What to show:** Screen recording of AutoFlow. Show you pasting a massive block of text, hitting "Run", and taking your hands off the keyboard.
- **What to say:** "But I use this Chrome extension called AutoFlow. I just dump my entire script into it—50 prompts at once. I set my resolution to 4K, hit run, and literally walk away. It types, clicks, waits, and even auto-retries if Google throws an error."

**Phase 4: The Proof (0:60 - 0:80)**
- **What to show:** The Library Scanner inside AutoFlow, selecting all videos, and hitting batch download. Show the folder full of MP4s.
- **What to say:** "When I come back, all 50 videos are done. I just hit 'Batch Download' and I have a whole movie's worth of B-roll ready to edit."

**Phase 5: The CTA (0:80 - 0:90)**
- **What to show:** The Chrome Web Store page for AutoFlow.
- **What to say:** "If you want to stop wasting time and put your AI video generation on autopilot, the extension is called AutoFlow. Link is in my bio and it's free to start."

## 📅 7 Video Ideas (Cycle through these every week)

To post every day, you need different angles. Rotate through these 7 concepts:

1. **The "Behind the Scenes" Angle:** Show how you made a specific short film using AutoFlow to generate all the scenes.
2. **The "Money Maker" Angle:** Focus on freelancers. "How to fulfill a client's 10-minute AI video order in 1 hour instead of 10 hours."
3. **The "Prompt Dump" Angle:** Share 10 amazing prompts on screen, then show how AutoFlow runs them all simultaneously.
4. **The "Feature Spotlight" Angle:** Focus entirely on the *Reference Image Mapping*. Show how you can keep one character consistent across 20 scenes automatically.
5. **The "Error Handling" Angle:** Show Google Flow failing (which happens a lot) and show AutoFlow automatically catching the error and retrying without you being there.
6. **The "Comparison" Angle:** Time-lapse of doing it manually vs doing it with AutoFlow side-by-side.
7. **The "Aesthetic" Angle:** Just 60 seconds of beautiful AI video, followed by 30 seconds of "I generated all of this while asleep using AutoFlow."

## ✂️ Pro Editing Rules for Retention
- **No dead air:** Remove every single breath and pause from your voiceover.
- **Change the screen every 3 seconds:** Zoom in on a button, pan across the prompts, cut to a generated video. If the screen is static for more than 3 seconds, people swipe.
- **Add captions:** Use big, bold captions (like the Alex Hormozi style) so people watching on mute still know what's happening.
- **Use trending audio:** Put a trending TikTok/Insta song in the background, but turn its volume down to 10% so your voice is clear.
