## 🖥️ DEMO — Quick Tour (0:45–2:30)

### Create Tab

> "Here's how it works. Open Google Flow, then open AutoFlow's side panel. Choose your mode — text-to-video, image-to-video, or ingredients — and paste your prompts."
>
> "Each paragraph becomes a separate task. So if I paste 5 scenes from my script, AutoFlow parses them into 5 individual prompts, ready to go."

**[SCREEN: Show pasting prompts → Parse Prompts → 5 prompts parsed]**

### Queue

> "Hit 'Add to Queue' and you get a queue card that shows everything at a glance — your video model, orientation, generation count, timing, and download settings. All configurable."
>
> "Then you just hit Run."

**[SCREEN: Show queue card with settings → click Run]**

### Automation Running

> "Watch the Run Monitor. AutoFlow opens Flow's settings, types each prompt using human-like pacing, clicks Generate, waits for the video to finish, and moves to the next one."
>
> "You can Pause, Resume, Skip a prompt, or Stop the queue at any time. You're always in control."

**[SCREEN: Show Run Monitor with live logs, pause/resume buttons]**

### Library

> "When it's done, switch to the Library tab, click Scan Project, and see all your videos grouped by prompt. Select the ones you want, and batch download in 720p, 1080p, or 4K."

**[SCREEN: Show Library scan → grouped results → Select All → Download]**

## 🔑 KEY BENEFITS (2:30–3:15)

> "So why use AutoFlow?"
>
> **"Speed"** — What takes 2 hours manually takes 10 minutes with AutoFlow.
>
> **"Reliability"** — Auto-retry on failures. If a video fails, AutoFlow retries it automatically.
>
> **"Organization"** — Every video is named by prompt, numbered by scene. No more 'download (47).mp4'.
>
> **"Flexibility"** — Works with text-to-video, image-to-video, frame chains, and ingredients mode."
