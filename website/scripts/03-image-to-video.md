## 📌 INTRO (0:10–0:35)

> "Google Flow's image-to-video mode is powerful, but attaching images to each prompt manually is painful. AutoFlow gives you three ways to do it — shared images, per-prompt images, and automatic character matching. Let's break them down."

---

## 🖥️ DEMO (0:35–2:45)

### Shared Reference Images

> "First: shared references. These are images that get attached to EVERY prompt automatically. Perfect for a consistent art style or environment reference."
>
> "Click '+ Add' under Shared Reference Images, select your image, and it's applied to all prompts in the queue."

**[SCREEN: Show Shared Reference Images → add an image → shows "1/3"]**

### Character Images

> "Next: character images. Toggle this on and AutoFlow will auto-match character images by name."
>
> "If your prompt says 'Floki standing near the barn', and you have a character named 'Floki' uploaded, AutoFlow automatically attaches Floki's reference image to that prompt."

**[SCREEN: Show Character Images toggle ON → add character → show matching]**

### Per-Prompt Images

> "Finally: per-prompt images. Each parsed prompt gets its own image slot — up to 10 images each. Click '+ Add images' on any prompt to attach specific references."

**[SCREEN: Show prompt list → click "+ Add images" on Scene 1 → upload]**

### Map Images to Prompts

> "There's also a bulk option: 'Upload & Map'. Upload a batch of images and AutoFlow maps image 1 to prompt 1, image 2 to prompt 2, and so on. Perfect when your images match your scene order."

**[SCREEN: Show "Map Images to Prompts" → Upload & Map → images distributed]**

---

## 💡 WHEN TO USE WHAT (2:45–3:15)

> "Quick summary:"
>
> | Method           | Best For                              |
> | ---------------- | ------------------------------------- |
> | **Shared**       | Same style/environment for ALL videos |
> | **Character**    | Consistent characters across scenes   |
> | **Per-prompt**   | Unique images for each scene          |
> | **Upload & Map** | Bulk — when images match prompt order |
